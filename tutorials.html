Some Basic tutorials

1.Banking Explained:

Banking refers to the business of accepting deposits, providing loans, and other financial services to individuals, businesses, and governments. Banks play a vital role in facilitating economic transactions, managing financial risks, and supporting economic growth.


2.Savings Account:

A savings bank is a type of financial institution that specializes in accepting and safeguarding savings deposits. Unlike commercial banks, which offer a wide range of financial services, savings banks primarily focus on providing a secure place for individuals to deposit their savings while earning some interest.


3.Current Account:

A current account, also known as a checking account in some regions, is a type of deposit account offered by banks and other financial institutions. It is designed for frequent and regular transactions, providing a convenient way for individuals and businesses to manage their day-to-day financial activities. Here are key features and aspects related to current accounts.


4.Salary Account:

A salary account is a type of bank account specifically designed to receive salary payments from an employer. This type of account is often offered by banks as a part of their services to both individuals and businesses. Here are key features and aspects related to salary accounts.


5.Indian Government Financial Schemes:

Indian government has introduced various financial schemes to promote economic development, financial inclusion, social welfare, and support specific sectors..


6.How to prevent Online Frauds:

Online frauds in India have become a significant concern with the increasing digitalization of services. These scams encompass a range of deceptive practices aimed at exploiting individuals, businesses, and financial institutions. Common online frauds in India include phishing attacks, identity theft, online banking fraud, investment scams, and e-commerce fraud.
